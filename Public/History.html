The Legacy of St. Mary's School

Founded in **1905**, St. Mary's School began with a mission to provide quality education and moral guidance to the community's youth.

The Early Years (1905-1940)

Starting as a small parish school with just 20 students, the school quickly established a reputation for discipline and academic rigor, rooted in the principles of our founding order.

Early curriculum focused on foundational literacy, arithmetic, and moral science. The original building, still standing today, served as the primary classroom and living quarters for the first faculty.

Post-War Growth (1940-1990)

Following WWII, St. Mary's experienced massive expansion, including the addition of a full science wing, a gymnasium, and the establishment of its first high school graduating class in 1952.

This period solidified our commitment to holistic education, introducing competitive sports teams, arts programs, and early computer literacy classes.

The Modern Era (1990-Present)

Today, St. Mary's is a technology leader, integrating smartboards, digital libraries, and advanced coding curriculum into every subject.

Our focus remains on preparing students for the global challenges of the 21st century, combining timeless values with cutting-edge academic resources.

Mission Statement
